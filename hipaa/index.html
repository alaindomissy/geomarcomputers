HIPAA ... what is it?

HIPAA Stands for Health Insurance Portability & Accountability Act. It is federal law for protecting patient information while it is stored and transferred. Read its history and background.

Who needs to be concerned with HIPAA?
Health care providers and people who do business with them. See a more detailed list here.

What is the scope of business functions impacted by HIPAA?
There are 5 areas which impact businesses - the EDI, the codes the unique identifiers, security and the admin Read the full article here.

How do I comply with HIPAA?
Affected providers must put business systems in place, and many requirements must be met by October 16, 2003. See a more detailed list here.

What is my next step to address the HIPAA regulations?
Contact a local HIPAA provider as soon as possible to help you assess, plan and implement the systems by October 16, 2003.

 
 

The Law was passed in 1996, but on April 14, 2003 HIPAA requrements began coming due.

Now is the time that health care providers are supposed to test and implement their HIPAA program and proceedures. Most of these must be complete, functional and in place by October 16, 2003.
